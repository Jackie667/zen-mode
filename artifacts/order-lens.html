Legal Clarity
Without a Law Degree

Legal problems don't care if you're a lawyer. But legal outcomes do favour the prepared. This isn't about replacing your lawyer — it's about not going in blind and paying expert rates to explain the basics.

This is for the person dealing with a landlord dispute, a contract they can't understand, a business disagreement, or an employment issue — who needs to act but doesn't know where to start. AI doesn't give legal advice. It gives you the clarity to ask the right questions.

⚠️ Important Limitations — Read This

  • AI cannot give legal advice. It can explain, organise, and clarify — but it cannot tell you what to do in your specific legal situation. Only a qualified lawyer can do that.
  • Jurisdiction is everything. Law varies by country, region, and contract type. Always verify that any AI output applies to your specific jurisdiction.
  • Don't use AI to generate legal documents without review. An AI-drafted contract may look correct and be legally hollow. Have a lawyer review before you sign or send.
  • Confidentiality matters. Don't share sensitive case details with public AI tools. Use enterprise-grade tools with privacy protections, or anonymise key details.
  • Complex matters need real lawyers. Criminal charges, immigration, high-value disputes, family law — these are not areas where AI preparation replaces professional representation.
